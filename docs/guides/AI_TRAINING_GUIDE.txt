# Loveca AI Training Guide

## 1. How to Start Training
Running the training script uses 12 cores at 50% load and stays within 8GB RAM.

```powershell
# Set path and run the optimized training script
$env:PYTHONPATH="."; uv run ai/train_optimized.py
```

## 2. Training Curriculum (The Road to Master)
Since you have no game data, follow this progression:

*   **Step 1: Learning the Rules (0 - 500k steps)**
    Training on simple decks. The AI learns color matching and basic math.
*   **Step 2: Exploring Abilities (500k - 2M steps)**
    Training on random "normal" decks. The AI discovers the power of "Draw" and "Bonus Score" abilities.
*   **Step 3: Meta Discovery (2M+ steps)**
    The AI practices against its own previous versions to find winning combos.

## 3. Will it create its own decks?
**Currently: No. It uses "Random Decks" generated by the engine.**
*   Every time an environment resets, the engine picks 48 random members and 12 random lives.
*   **Why?** This makes the AI "General Purpose." It learns which card attributes (like "Green Heart" or "Draw 2") are good in *any* situation, rather than just memorizing one specific deck.

## 4. How does it know if a card/combo is good?
The AI uses a **Reward Signal**:
*   **Win/Loss**: +1.0 for winning, -1.0 for losing.
*   **Score Difference**: +0.1 for every point of lead it has over the opponent.
*   **Illegal Moves**: Massive penalty (-100) if it tries to break a rule (though MaskablePPO prevents this).
*   **Observation**: The AI "sees" its hand, the stage, and the scores. If it plays Card A and Card B together and its win rate spikes, the Neural Network strengthens the connection between "See A+B" and "Play A+B."

## 5. Logging & Monitoring
We have two layers of logging:

1.  **Terminal Output (Standard)**: Shows "mean reward" and "fps". If mean reward goes up, the AI is getting smarter.
2.  **TensorBoard (Visual)**:
    *   Logs are saved to `./logs/ppo_tensorboard/`.
    *   Run `tensorboard --logdir ./logs/ppo_tensorboard/` to see graphs of win rates and learning progress in your browser.
3.  **Win Rate Tracker**: I am adding a simple win-rate counter to the environment so you can see live performance in the terminal.
